Gulab Jamun

Description

Gulab Jamun is a beloved Indian sweet delicacy enjoyed across the Indian subcontinent and beyond.
These round, soft, and spongy dessert balls are made from a dough consisting mainly of milk solids, traditionally khoya (reduced milk solids), mixed with a little flour and a leavening agent such as baking soda.

Ingredients

  • Milk solids (khoya/mawa)
  • All-purpose flour
  • Baking soda
  • Sugar
  • Water
  • Rose water
  • Cardamom pods (optional)
  • Saffron strands (optional)
  • Ghee or oil for frying
  • Chopped nuts (pistachios, almonds) for garnish

Steps

  1. Mix khoya, flour, and baking soda to make dough.
  2. Shape dough into small balls.
  3. Fry the balls until golden brown.
  4. Boil sugar, water, and flavorings to make syrup.
  5. Soak fried balls in syrup for a few hours.
  6. Garnish with chopped nuts before serving.
